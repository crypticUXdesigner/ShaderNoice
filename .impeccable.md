## Design Context

### Users
ShaderNoice is primarily for **hobby / exploratory generative artists** creating procedural shader art through a node graph. Their context is playful experimentation and creative exploration rather than production pipelines, but they still benefit from editor-grade clarity and predictability.

The UI should optimize for **learning and discovery of nodes**: understanding what nodes do, finding the right node quickly, and confidently wiring graphs without fear of “breaking things”.

### Brand Personality
**Creative, playful, calm.**

- Creative: invites experimentation and iteration.
- Playful: small moments of personality are welcome, but never childish.
- Calm: reduces cognitive load; avoids visual noise, harsh contrast spikes, or anxiety-inducing alerts.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-only (intentional). Strong readability, editor-like density where needed.
- **Core palette**: Teal/blue accents remain the primary interaction and emphasis colors.
- **Visual language**: Technical creative tool, closer to TouchDesigner/Ableton sensibilities than “SaaS dashboards”.
- **Motion**: Subtle polish (small, purposeful transitions), with first-class support for `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- **Density**: Adaptive — breathing room for reading surfaces (docs/dialogs/help), compact for tool surfaces (dense lists, parameter controls).

**Anti-references (explicitly avoid):**
- Generic SaaS dashboards / corporate UI
- Cartoony / childish visuals
- Skeuomorphic “OS widget” gloss
- Over-minimal UIs that hide power
- Neon-rave/cyberpunk overload
- macOS Aqua nostalgia

### Design Principles
1. **Discovery-first**: help users find, understand, and choose nodes quickly (search, categorization, docs, examples).
2. **Calm confidence**: reduce cognitive load with consistent tokens, predictable layouts, and gentle emphasis.
3. **Editor-grade affordances**: dense where it helps, but always legible; prioritize clear states (focus/hover/selected/connected).
4. **Purposeful motion**: use motion only as feedback and orientation; honor reduced-motion preferences.
5. **Accent discipline**: reserve strong teal/blue emphasis for meaning (primary actions, selection, connected/animated state), not decoration.

## Design Rules (encoded in the codebase)

### Tokens & scales (source of truth)
- **Scales** live in `src/styles/scales.css` (spacing, radius, text sizes, color scales).
- **DOM UI tokens** live in `src/styles/tokens.css` (buttons, inputs, overlays, layout surfaces).
- **Node editor tokens** live in `src/styles/tokens-node-editor.css` (canvas/grid, nodes, ports, connections, parameter UI).

**Rule of thumb:** prefer tokens; introduce new tokens only when semantic/reused/JS-consumed. Keep “accent discipline”: strong teal/blue emphasis should communicate state/meaning.

### Density system
- **Reading surfaces** (docs panels, help, dialogs, empty states): prefer comfortable spacing and longer line-height.
- **Tool surfaces** (parameter editors, node lists, dense menus): prefer compact spacing and tighter grouping, but never at the expense of clarity.
- Prefer **one dense region inside a calm frame** (e.g. dense list inside a panel section) over making every surface dense.

### Motion system
- Motion is **feedback and orientation**, not decoration.
- Always honor `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` (global baseline in `src/styles/reduced-motion.css`).

### Copy & tone
- Tone is **gentle guide**: short, supportive, non-judgmental.
- “Playful” is allowed only in **microcopy** and **subtle motion**. Avoid childish jokes or loud personality.

### Icons
- Keep icons consistent with **Phosphor defaults** (weight/stroke, proportions).
- If new custom icons are added, they must match existing stroke/radius conventions (see icon tokens in `tokens-node-editor.css`).

### Accessibility (baseline)
- Default to **balanced contrast** on dark surfaces (avoid ultra-dim “cinematic” UI that harms readability).
- Reduced-motion support is required; focus/selection states must remain clear with motion off.
